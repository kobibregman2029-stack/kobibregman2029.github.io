The Elephant

African elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. Adult males can weigh 10,000–14,000 pounds and stand up to 13 feet tall at the shoulder.
Asian elephants are smaller. Adult males usually weigh 6,000–11,000 pounds and stand about 8–10 feet tall.

Elephants are herbivores, meaning they eat grasses, leaves, bark, roots, and fruit.
An adult elephant can eat 200–300 pounds of food per day and drink up to 50 gallons of water daily.

Elephants typically live 60–70 years in the wild.
