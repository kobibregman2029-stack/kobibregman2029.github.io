The Lion

The lion's scientific name is Panthera leo. It belongs to the genus Panthera, which includes tigers, leopards, and jaguars.

Adult male lions typically weigh about 330–550 pounds (150–250 kg). Adult females usually weigh about 265–395 pounds (120–180 kg).

Lions are obligate carnivores, meaning they must eat meat to survive. Their digestive systems are adapted to process animal protein and fat, not plant material.

They eat the following animals, but that's just a few of hundreds:
